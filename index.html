22 a Day

Since 2010, June was acknowledged as National PTSD Awareness Month with June 27th as PTSD Awareness Day. In 2016, the VA recently released a report that suggests that on average, as many as 22 veterans commit suicide every day.  These visualizations serve to give credence to this number and build an intuition for the relative  severity of this situation. For a more detailed look at the numbers, simply hover over what you are  interested in looking at!

About these visualizations

For this dataset, I chose to create an interactive slideshow where hovering over specific parts  of the visualization triggers an tooltip for that respective section, giving greater detail for  whatever is being looked at.

Scene transitions are facilitated by simply using "Next" and "Previous" buttons.  These buttons also serve as parameters for the scenes, where instead of explicitly stating  a slide number, the first and last slides only having "Next" and "Previous" buttons,  respectively, to signify start and end. Given the short length of the presentation, I thought  this design choice appropriate.

Each slide has an explanation of the intent of what is being shown, outlining what data being shown. The first slide serves as an explanation of what they are about to see and general instructions for  interacting with the visualizations. The instructions for interactions are reiterated again in the  slide with the map, where I felt the interaction served a greater purpose than in the others.
